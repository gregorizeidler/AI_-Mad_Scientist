🧪 Temperature Recommendations by Theme

Theoretical Physics and Cosmology

Recommended Temperature: High (0.9-1.5)

Themes like black holes, string theory, multiverses, and quantum physics benefit from high temperatures to generate truly innovative hypotheses.

Futuristic Technologies

Recommended Temperature: High (0.9-1.2)

AI, nanotechnology, advanced biotechnology, and quantum computing work well with high creativity to imagine technological futures.

Biology and Ecology

Recommended Temperature: Medium (0.6-0.8)

Themes about evolution, ecosystems, microbiology, and adaptation work better with a balance between innovation and plausibility.

Psychology and Behavior

Recommended Temperature: Medium (0.5-0.7)

Theories about mind, behavior, and cognition need creativity but also consistency with existing knowledge.

Engineering and Design

Recommended Temperature: Medium-Low (0.4-0.6)

Practical projects and engineering solutions require more focus on viability and implementation.

Mathematics and Logic

Recommended Temperature: Low (0.3-0.5)

Mathematical theories benefit from greater precision and logical consistency, even when exploring advanced concepts.

🔄 Dynamic Temperature Adjustment

To get the best results, consider dynamically adjusting agent temperatures based on the theme and intermediate results:

Recommended Strategies

  • Start high, end low: Begin with high temperature for initial generation and progressively reduce for refinement.
  • Feedback cycle: If critical analysis finds many problems, generate a new theory with slightly reduced temperature.
  • Dynamic balance: Adjust temperatures until you find the "sweet spot" where ideas are creative but also coherent.
  • Controlled experimentation: Keep all variables constant and vary only one agent's temperature at a time to understand its impact.
